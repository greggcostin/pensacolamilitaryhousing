PCS Checklist — 60/30/7 Day Timeline for Military Families

The 60/30/7 PCS checklist I wish I had on my first move. Built from 11 personal PCS moves and hundreds of client closings tied to military orders.

Every PCS is different. Every PCS is the same. This checklist is the practical timeline that works whether you're OCONUS inbound to Pensacola or CONUS hopping one last time before retirement in Destin. I've done 11 of them. My clients have done hundreds more under my guidance. The order below is what keeps families from missing deadlines, forfeiting money, or showing up homeless at the new duty station.

60 Days Before Report Date

Orders and Finance

  • Download signed orders from myPay / AMS / MyNavy Portal. Save five copies — physical and digital.
  • Pull your Certificate of Eligibility (COE) for VA loan use. If you have never pulled it, do this today — it takes 10 minutes on VA.gov.
  • Contact a VA-specialist lender for pre-approval. NOT a retail national lender. Pre-approval takes 5-10 business days.
  • Verify DLA (Dislocation Allowance) and TLE (Temporary Lodging Expense) entitlements for your rank and family size.
  • If DITY/PPM: request weight ticket procedures and household goods weight allowance from your Transportation Management Office (TMO).

Housing

  • Start virtual home tours in your target neighborhoods. Filter by commute to your new duty station and school zones.
  • If buying: get pre-approval letter from VA-specialist lender in hand before you tour.
  • If renting: get on wait lists for Balfour Beatty Communities or equivalent base housing at NAS Pensacola, Hurlburt, or Eglin.
  • Compare FL064 (Pensacola) vs FL023 (Fort Walton Beach) BAH rates for your duty station.

Family and Schools

  • Pull last 2 years of school transcripts, IEP/504 plans, immunization records for every school-age child.
  • Contact receiving school district military liaison — all Escambia, Santa Rosa, and Okaloosa county schools have one.
  • If spouse is employed: start remote-work conversation or state-to-state license transfer research.

30 Days Before Report Date

Housing Execution

  • If buying: write offers on your top 2-3 homes. VA appraisal takes 10-14 days after contract acceptance.
  • If selling a current home: list 30-45 days before the PCS move if possible — photography, pricing, listing prep takes 1-2 weeks.
  • Lock interest rate with your lender for 45-60 days.
  • Schedule home inspection (typically within 7-10 days of offer acceptance).

Household Goods Move

  • Confirm HHG pickup date with TMO. Typical range: 14-21 days before report date.
  • If DITY/PPM: obtain weight ticket paperwork and rental truck reservation.
  • Prepare high-value inventory (HVI) list for movers — firearms, jewelry, electronics serial numbers, heirlooms. Photograph everything.
  • Separate "do not pack" zone — passports, orders, prescriptions, laptop, tools, first-aid kit.

Administrative

  • Out-process appointments: dental, medical, finance, education, legal.
  • TRICARE region transfer: update region, confirm PCM availability at new location.
  • Forward mail via USPS Change of Address (effective date = report date).
  • Notify insurance (auto, renters/homeowners, life) of the move and new address.

7 Days Before Report Date

Final Housing Steps

  • If buying: confirm closing date, schedule final walk-through, wire earnest money and closing costs to title company.
  • Schedule utility transfers: electric, water, internet, trash — typical 48-72 hour lead time.
  • Confirm Balfour Beatty move-in or temp-lodging reservation.

Final Move Logistics

  • Pack personal vehicles with essentials — clothes, documents, kids' comfort items, 3 days of meds.
  • Confirm HHG inventory sheet matches what movers loaded. Sign for accuracy.
  • Complete final walkthrough of old quarters/home. Photograph every room before departure.
  • Turn in base housing keys / finalize lease end-of-term paperwork.

Arrival Prep

  • Confirm in-processing appointments at new duty station — finance, IDs, schools, TRICARE PCM.
  • Register for Florida driver's license within 30 days of arrival (military exemption for out-of-state license as long as orders are current, but Florida residency is often financially preferable).
  • Vehicle registration: 10-day grace period after establishing FL residency.
  • File Florida Homestead Exemption by March 1 of the year following purchase — this is the single largest tax break available to Florida homeowners.

Arrival Day and Beyond

  • Walk through new home with movers present. Note any damage on the delivery paperwork before signing off.
  • Save every receipt related to the move — DITY/PPM reimbursement, TLE, mileage, lodging, meals.
  • Submit move claims within 75 days of delivery per the Defense Personal Property System (DPS).
  • Schedule school enrollment with transcripts, IEP/504 plans, immunization records in hand.
  • Enroll in military family readiness programs at the new base — MWR, spouse employment, youth activities.

Common Pensacola-Area PCS Mistakes

  • Assuming NAS Pensacola and Hurlburt have the same BAH. They don't. FL064 and FL023 are different MHAs with different rates. I have seen families lose $200-400/mo of BAH by picking the wrong side of the Pensacola Bay.
  • Not verifying school zones at the parcel level. Escambia County has boundary lines that split streets. A "Bellview Middle" listing may actually feed into Beulah Middle.
  • Skipping Homestead Exemption. If you don't file by March 1 of the year following purchase, you forfeit up to $50,000 of assessed-value exemption — $800-1,200 per year in lost tax savings.
  • Using a retail national lender instead of a VA-specialist. Retail lenders often miss VA-specific cost structures, slow-walk VA appraisals, and occasionally fail to waive the funding fee for disabled veterans at closing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I start a PCS move?

The moment you have signed orders in hand: pull your VA Certificate of Eligibility, get VA-specialist lender pre-approval (5-10 business days), start virtual home tours, contact your new duty station's sponsor program, and begin out-processing appointments. 60 days before your report-no-later-than date is the minimum comfortable start; 90 days is better.

How long does a PCS move take?

From receipt of orders to arrival at the new duty station, typical PCS timeline is 60-90 days. The HHG (household goods) shipment itself takes 10-30 days depending on origin and destination — OCONUS moves can exceed 45 days. Build 2-4 weeks of buffer between movers pickup and arrival at new duty station for a realistic plan.

What does PCS stand for in the military?

Permanent Change of Station. A PCS move is a long-duration reassignment to a new duty station, typically 2-4 years. Unlike TDY (temporary duty), a PCS moves your family, household goods, and legal residence. The government reimburses DLA (Dislocation Allowance) and covers HHG shipment via Transportation Management Office (TMO).

What if my orders are delayed or revoked?

VA loan offers should include an orders contingency clause that protects you if orders change. Most VA sellers and their agents understand this and accept it. I write this clause into every military-client offer by default.

Should I rent or buy on my first PCS to Pensacola?

Depends on assignment length, rank, and whether you plan to keep the home as a rental after PCS. For 3+ year assignments with a VA loan and a long-term rental exit strategy, buying is almost always the stronger financial move. For under-12-month schools and short-pipeline assignments, renting usually wins.

How do I coordinate HHG delivery with home closing?

If HHG arrives before closing, request storage from TMO (covered under your entitlement for up to 90 days). If closing is delayed, HHG can sit in storage at no additional cost. The sequence that fails most often: movers arrive on closing day while you are still at the title company. Build 3-5 days of buffer between closing and scheduled HHG delivery.

Can I close on a home before I arrive in Pensacola?

Yes. Remote closings with power of attorney are routine for military buyers, especially OCONUS. I handle the POA coordination with the closing attorney and notarize through the American Embassy or base legal services in your current location.

How much does the military pay for a PCS move?

Varies by rank, dependent status, and origin-to-destination distance. Core entitlements: Dislocation Allowance (DLA) $1,200-$4,800 depending on rank and dependents; TLE (Temporary Lodging Expense) up to 14 days CONUS or 60 days OCONUS; per diem for the en-route days; mileage reimbursement (MALT) $0.22/mile; HHG shipment fully covered under weight allowance. DITY/PPM (Personally Procured Move) reimburses 100% of the government-constructed cost if you move yourself.
