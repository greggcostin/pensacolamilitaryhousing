Bellview & Myrtle Grove — West Pensacola Starter-Home Value for Military Buyers

Adjacent working-class neighborhoods 10-15 minutes from the NAS Pensacola West Gate with the strongest E-3 to E-5 starter-home VA loan math in the Pensacola metro.

Bellview and Myrtle Grove are adjacent unincorporated Escambia County neighborhoods in west Pensacola, immediately north of NAS Pensacola between Blue Angel Parkway and Pensacola Boulevard. They are commonly grouped together because they share overlapping school zoning and similar housing stock — 1970s-80s ranch homes, some newer infill, working-class character. For a young enlisted service member buying a first home with a VA loan, no other neighborhood in the Pensacola metro delivers a shorter commute at a lower price point. This is the starter-home market.

Commute to NAS Pensacola: 10-15 min via Blue Angel Pkwy
Commute to Corry Station: 15-20 min
Commute to downtown Pensacola: 10-15 min
School District: Escambia County
BAH MHA: FL064 — Pensacola
Median home price (2026): $185,000–$290,000
Typical VA loan fit: E-3 and above (starter-home market)
Character: 1970s-80s ranch · newer infill

Why Bellview / Myrtle Grove for Starter-Home Military Buyers

Three reasons: price, commute, and VA loan math. Median prices run $185K-$290K, which means E-3 and E-4 BAH can actually support a 3BR home with a VA loan — rare in Pensacola. Commute to NAS Pensacola West Gate is 10-15 minutes, among the shortest in the metro. And a $225K home on a VA loan with zero down preserves BAH as equity rather than rent. If your orders are 3+ years and your target is a home you can eventually rent out or sell when you PCS, Bellview and Myrtle Grove are the strongest starter plays in the market.

Streets and Sub-Areas

Bellview proper (east side, near Mobile Hwy)

Older established. Mid-century ranch, 1970s-80s homes. $195K-$285K.

Myrtle Grove proper (south side, closer to base)

Slightly newer, some updated inventory, shortest NAS Pensacola commute. $210K-$305K.

North Bellview / Sorrento corridor

Newer infill and renovated ranch homes. $245K-$335K. Best for E-5+ who want more updated inventory.

BAH and Purchase Price

FL064 Pensacola MHA. E-3 BAH with dependents ($1,794) supports $230K-$250K with conservative underwriting — meaningful Bellview and Myrtle Grove inventory available. E-4 (also $1,794) same range. E-5 ($1,863) clears most of the market. E-6 ($2,235) has the full run. For dual-income military families, the BAH math here is so favorable that the second income can go entirely to savings and investment.

Schools

Escambia County Public Schools. Bellview Elementary and Myrtle Grove Elementary serve their respective zones, with Bellview Middle as the typical middle school. High school is typically Escambia High or Pine Forest High depending on street. School quality varies meaningfully by campus — I zone every property to its specific elementary/middle/high assignment and pull state DOE data before offer. If top-tier schools are the priority, Gulf Breeze (Santa Rosa County) is the stronger call even though the commute triples.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where are Bellview and Myrtle Grove?

Bellview and Myrtle Grove are adjacent unincorporated Escambia County neighborhoods in west Pensacola, between Blue Angel Parkway and Pensacola Boulevard, roughly 10-15 minutes from the NAS Pensacola West Gate. They are commonly grouped together in the local market because of overlapping school zoning and similar housing stock.

How affordable are Bellview and Myrtle Grove?

Very. Median home prices run $185K-$290K for 3BR inventory, which is among the lowest in the Pensacola metro. E-3 and E-4 BAH with dependents ($1,794 in FL064 for 2026) can support meaningful inventory here. For first-time VA buyers, this is the most accessible starter-home market near NAS Pensacola.

How is the commute to NAS Pensacola?

10-15 minutes to the West Gate via Blue Angel Parkway. Rush hour adds 5 minutes. This is one of the shortest commutes in the Pensacola MHA, which matters when you balance duty schedules and family logistics.

What schools serve Bellview and Myrtle Grove?

Escambia County Public Schools. Bellview Elementary and Myrtle Grove Elementary serve their respective zones. Middle school zoning is typically Bellview Middle. High school is typically Escambia High or Pine Forest High depending on street. School quality varies by campus; I zone every property to its specific school before offer.

Should I buy a starter home here or rent until I can afford Gulf Breeze?

That depends on your orders length and financial picture. For a 3+ year NAS Pensacola assignment, buying a $225K starter home in Myrtle Grove on a VA loan and keeping your BAH working as principal is often a better financial move than renting until you can stretch to Gulf Breeze. Equity plus tax benefits plus no PMI usually wins over a two-year wait. I run the full rent-vs-buy math before you decide.
